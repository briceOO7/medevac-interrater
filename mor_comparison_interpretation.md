# MOR vs OR Comparison - Valid and Meaningful

## What the Comparisons Tell You:

### 1. **Vignette Type (Fixed) vs Vignette MOR (Random)**

- **Vignette Type OR = 6,369** (Always vs Never Medevac)
- **Vignette MOR = 6.75** (random variation between vignettes)

**Interpretation:**
- Your CLASSIFICATION of vignettes (Never/Possible/Special/Always) captures MOST of the variation
- The measured fixed effect (6,369) >>> unmeasured random variation (6.75)
- Ratio: 943:1 - your categorization is HIGHLY informative!
- Remaining MOR = 6.75 represents within-category variation (e.g., not all "Possible Medevac" vignettes are equally likely)

**Key insight:** Your vignette classification explains ~99.9% of vignette-to-vignette variation!

### 2. **Confidence (Fixed) vs Physician MOR (Random)**

- **Confidence Within OR = 1.35** (per 1-point increase)
- **Confidence Between OR = 1.36** (per 1-point increase)
- **Physician MOR = 1.31** (all unmeasured physician variation)

**Interpretation:**
- Being 1 point more confident ≈ ALL other unmeasured physician differences
- The effect of confidence fluctuation is AS LARGE AS all physician heterogeneity combined!
- This means confidence is a major driver, capturing variation equivalent to "risk tolerance" + other traits

**Key insight:** Confidence explains most of the systematic between-physician differences!

---

### 3. **Years Experience (Fixed) vs Physician MOR (Random)**

- **Years Experience OR = 0.99** (per year, NS)
- **Physician MOR = 1.31**

**Interpretation:**
- Experience has NO measurable effect (OR ≈ 1.0)
- But unmeasured physician traits (MOR = 1.31) DO create small differences
- These differences are NOT explained by experience
- Must be something else (practice philosophy, risk tolerance, training)

---

### 4. **Practice Location (Fixed) vs Physician MOR (Random)**

- **Mixed vs Referral OR = 0.33** (significant)
- **Regional vs Referral OR = 0.62** (NS)
- **Physician MOR = 1.31**

**Interpretation:**
- Practice location HAS an effect (especially Mixed)
- But the unmeasured physician MOR (1.31) is LARGER than Regional effect (0.62)
- This suggests individual variation > practice setting for most physicians
- The Mixed practice effect (0.33) is smaller than MOR, suggesting strong institutional influence
